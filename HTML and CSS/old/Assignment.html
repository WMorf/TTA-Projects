These are my thoughts on currency scaling in Table Top Roleplaying Games.

Coinage refers to the metal pieces often found by adventurers as treasure and used to purchase goods and services. The standard currency in most worlds of Dungeons & Dragons is the gold piece (abbreviated gp), although platinum, silver, copper, and coinage of other materials are also found as treasure.
Master of coin CFO
Copper

The penny of the fantasy world.

Silver

Much more shiny. Basically a token for a good meal. Worth 10 Copper coins.

Electrum

Electrum is a naturally occurring alloy of gold and silver, with trace amounts of copper and other metals. Worth 5 Silver coins.

Gold

Shiny,shimmering splendor. Once you start throwing these around, you'll forget what copper looks like. Worth 10 Silver coins.

Platinum

Cause you always have to go one extra. Strange this is on the top when electrum contains trace amounts of platinum typically. Ah well. Worth 10 Gold coins.
